Combinatorial Optimization with Graph Neural Networks

Reproduces and extends the GCON framework (Wenkel et al., 2024) — an unsupervised GNN that equips each node with richer feature representations through a filter bank of aggregation and comparison filters. Extends to Maximum Independent Set and Balanced Graph Partitioning by deriving self-supervised loss functions and rule-based decoders for each. GCON outperforms ScatteringClique on Graph Partitioning and remains competitive on Maximum Independent Set.
